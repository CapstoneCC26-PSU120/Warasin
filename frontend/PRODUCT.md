# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Everyday individuals looking to check, monitor, and understand their stress levels. They might be feeling overwhelmed or just curious about their current mental state, so they need an interface that feels welcoming, unintimidating, and easy to use.

## Product Purpose
Warasin exists to provide users with a quick, approachable way to measure their stress. By taking a selfie or chatting with a chatbot, users receive immediate feedback on their stress levels and can track these measurements over time in a history dashboard. Success looks like a user feeling supported and informed, rather than diagnosed or anxious.

## Brand Personality
Calm, friendly, and approachable. The tone should be reassuring and supportive, avoiding sterile clinical language while remaining trustworthy. Inspiration taken from Hedepy, especially its clean and approachable typography.

## Anti-references
- Harsh, overly complex enterprise or B2B SaaS dashboards
- Cold, sterile medical or hospital-like interfaces
- Overwhelming, data-heavy screens that induce anxiety rather than reduce it

## Design Principles
- **A safe space:** The interface should feel like a deep breath—uncrowded, soft, and inviting.
- **Human-centric first:** Interactions (like the chatbot or selfie upload) should feel conversational and natural, not like filling out a tax form.
- **Clarity over density:** Present results and history clearly and gently. Avoid overwhelming the user with raw data; instead, focus on meaningful, digestible insights.
- **Reassuring feedback:** Every action should have a clear, calm response so the user always feels in control of their journey.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Standard best practices. Ensure good contrast for readability, straightforward navigation, and intuitive forms to reduce cognitive load, keeping the experience frictionless.
